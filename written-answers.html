North Star, Events & Research Plan

The metrics, instrumentation, and discovery questions behind Quarterstack.

North Star Metric

Time to Finalized Quarterly Plan

Average time a PM takes to go from raw backlog to a committed quarterly plan.

What this product actually does

✕ Store feature ideas
✕ Score features in a spreadsheet
✕ Generate pretty roadmaps
✓ Reduce decision-making time. Get from chaos to clarity, fast.

Why this, not something else

"Plan completion rate" is too binary

It tells you if they finished, not how fast. A PM who takes 3 days across 5 sessions technically "completed" the plan. That's not a win.

"Number of features planned" is a vanity metric

More features planned doesn't mean better planning. A PM who plans 5 high-impact features in 30 minutes beat one who planned 15 in 3 hours.

"Agent messages sent" measures engagement, not value

A PM chatting a lot with the AI might mean the AI is helpful, or it might mean they're confused. Activity is not the same as progress.

How we'd measure it

Track the timestamp of the PM's first interaction (opening the tool, expanding a card, moving the cut line) and the timestamp of their last meaningful action before the plan reaches a "finalized" state. Finalized = features above the cut line, total effort fits within the quarter's capacity, and no changes made for 60+ seconds. The delta between first and last action is the metric.

5 Events I'd Instrument

1

cut_line_moved

critical

Fires when the PM drags the cut line to a new position.

Why: This is the hero interaction. Every cut line move is a scope decision. Frequency tells us if PMs are actively making choices or just looking at the defaults. If nobody moves it, the default position is either perfect or the interaction isn't clear enough.

properties: old_position, new_position, features_above, features_below, is_over_capacity
2

score_adjusted

signal

Fires when a PM changes any pre-filled score (Impact, Effort, or Confidence).

Why: Measures trust in the AI's pre-fill. If nobody adjusts scores, either the AI is perfect (unlikely) or PMs aren't engaging with the data. If everyone adjusts everything, the pre-fill is adding no value. The sweet spot is 20-30% adjustment rate. Also tells us which dimension PMs disagree with most (Impact vs Effort vs Confidence).

properties: feature_id, field (impact/effort/confidence), old_value, new_value, score_change_delta
3

plan_finalized

critical

Fires when the plan reaches a finalized state: features above the cut line, effort fits within the quarter, and no changes for 60+ seconds.

Why: This is the completion event. Without it, we can't measure the North Star metric. The time delta between session start and this event IS the "Time to Finalized Plan." Also captures: how many features made it in, total effort planned, and whether the PM was over capacity at any point during the session.

properties: session_duration_seconds, features_in_plan, total_effort_weeks, was_ever_over_capacity, score_adjustments_count
4

agent_message_sent

engagement

Fires when the PM sends a message to either the Analyst or PO agent.

Why: Tells us if the AI agents are part of the workflow or being ignored. Which agent gets more questions? What are PMs actually asking about? If Analyst gets 80% of messages, PMs need more data help than decision help. The message content (anonymized) reveals what questions we should be answering proactively instead of waiting for the PM to ask.

properties: agent_type (analyst/po), message_length, session_time_elapsed, features_expanded_before_asking
5

feature_deleted

signal

Fires when a PM removes a feature from the backlog entirely.

Why: Deletion is a stronger signal than moving below the cut line. Below the line means "not this quarter." Deleting means "this shouldn't be here at all." High deletion rates tell us the ingestion pipeline is sending noise. Low deletion rates mean curation is happening upstream, which is good. Also tracks whether the PM used the undo, which tells us if deletions are deliberate or accidental.

properties: feature_id, was_above_cut_line, feature_source, undo_used (boolean)

PM Interview Questions

Questions I'd ask PMs before building this product. Structured for a 30-minute interview. The goal is to understand how planning actually works today, where it breaks, and what role AI could play.

01

Current Workflow

How they plan today

Walk me through your last quarterly planning cycle, start to finish. How many days did it take?

Where did you spend the most time: gathering data, scoring features, or debating what makes the cut?

How many tools did you touch during the process? Can you list them?

Did the final plan match what you presented to stakeholders, or did it change after the review meeting?

02

Decision-Making

How they choose what's in vs out

When you had to cut a feature from the plan, how did you make that call? What data did you look at?

Do you use a scoring framework (RICE, ICE, etc.)? If yes, do you actually trust the output, or do you override it based on gut?

Think of a time you prioritized something that scored low on your framework but still felt right. What made you override the score?

03

Pain Points

Where it breaks

What's the most frustrating part of prioritization today?

Where do decisions get delayed the most? Is it missing data, too many opinions, or something else?

What kind of information is hardest to gather when you're scoring a feature?

What's one thing about quarterly planning you've just accepted as "that's how it is" but wish was different?

04

Tools & Data

What they use, what's missing

Where does customer feedback live today? How do you pull it into your planning process?

When you need data to support a prioritization decision (usage stats, support tickets, revenue impact), how long does it take to get it?

Do you have a single source of truth for your backlog, or is it scattered across Jira, Notion, Sheets, and Slack?

05

AI & Automation

Trust boundaries

If an AI could handle one part of your planning process for you, what would you hand off first?

Where would you still want full control, even if an AI could do it?

What would make you trust an automated prioritization system? What would break that trust?

Have you tried using ChatGPT or similar tools for planning? What worked and what didn't?

06

Collaboration & Stakeholders

Who's involved, where disagreements happen

Who else is involved in your quarterly planning? Engineering leads, design, executives?

When there's a disagreement about what to prioritize, how does it get resolved? Who has the final call?

How do you communicate the quarterly plan to the rest of the team? What format does it end up in?
