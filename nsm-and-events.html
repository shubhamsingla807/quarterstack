NSM & Event Instrumentation

Metrics, Events & Research Plan

How we'd measure whether Quarterstack is actually working, and the questions we'd ask before building it.

North Star Metric

Time to Finalized Quarterly Plan

Average time a PM takes to go from raw backlog to a committed quarterly plan.

This product is fundamentally about reducing decision latency. The North Star needs to reflect how quickly a PM can arrive at a confident plan.

What this product is really about

✕ Storing feature ideas
✕ Scoring features in a spreadsheet
✕ Generating pretty roadmaps
✓ Reducing decision-making time. Chaos to clarity, fast.

Why this, not something else

"Plan completion rate" is too binary

Tells you if they finished, not how fast. A PM who takes 3 days across 5 sessions technically "completed" the plan. That's not a win.

"Features planned" is vanity

More features doesn't mean better planning. 5 high-impact features in 30 minutes beats 15 in 3 hours.

"Agent messages sent" measures activity, not value

Lots of chat might mean the AI is helpful, or it might mean the PM is confused. Activity is not progress.

How we'd measure it

Start

First meaningful interaction: backlog opened, card expanded, or cut line moved.

End

Plan reaches a stable finalized state:

  • Features above the cut line
  • Total effort fits within the quarter
  • No significant changes over a short period
Metric

Time delta between these two points.

Guardrail: Plan Stability / Rework Rate

Speed without quality is meaningless. To make sure faster planning doesn't mean sloppy planning, I'd track:

% of features changed after finalization

If PMs keep coming back to rearrange the plan after "finalizing" it, they either rushed or the tool gave them false confidence. A healthy rework rate is under 15%. Above that, the plan wasn't really done.

5 Events I'd Instrument

1

planning_session_started

baseline

Fires on first meaningful interaction: card expanded, score adjusted, or cut line moved.

Why: This is the start timestamp for the NSM. Without it, we can't measure "time to plan." Also tells us how many sessions get abandoned before completion, which is a churn signal for the tool itself.

properties: backlog_size, features_pre_scored, timestamp
2

cut_line_moved

critical

Fires when the PM drags the cut line to a new position.

Why: This is the hero interaction. Every move is a scope decision. If nobody moves it, PMs are either accepting defaults blindly or the interaction isn't clear. High move frequency = active decision-making.

properties: old_position, new_position, features_above, is_over_capacity
3

plan_finalized

critical

Fires when the plan reaches a stable state: features above the line, effort within capacity, no more changes.

Why: The end timestamp for the NSM. Time delta between planning_session_started and this event IS the North Star metric. Also captures plan shape: how many features, total effort, whether they went over capacity at any point during the session.

properties: session_duration_sec, features_in_plan, total_effort_weeks, score_adjustments_count, was_ever_over_capacity
4

ai_suggestion_accepted

impact

Fires when a PM acts on an AI agent's recommendation: keeps a pre-filled score, moves a feature the PO agent flagged, or follows a trade-off suggestion.

Why: Measures whether AI is actually reducing effort, not just being used. A PM who chats with the agent 20 times but ignores every suggestion gets zero value. This event tracks real impact. Also breaks down by agent type (Analyst vs PO) to show which agent drives more action.

properties: agent_type, suggestion_type (score/trade-off/swap), was_chat_initiated, time_to_accept_sec
5

score_adjusted

signal

Fires when a PM changes any pre-filled score (Impact, Effort, or Confidence).

Why: Trust signal for the AI pre-fill. Ideal adjustment rate is 20-30%. Much higher means the AI is off. Much lower means PMs aren't engaging. Also tells us which dimension gets overridden most, so we can improve the model there first.

properties: feature_id, field (impact/effort/confidence), old_value, new_value, delta

PM Interview Questions

Structured for a 30-minute conversation. Goal: understand how planning actually works, where it breaks, and where AI fits.

01

Current Workflow

Walk me through your last quarterly planning cycle. How many days did it take, start to finish?

Where did you spend the most time: gathering data, scoring things, or debating what makes the cut?

How many tools did you touch during the process? What did each one do?

Where does customer feedback, support data, and sales input actually live? How do you pull it into your planning?

02

Decision-Making

When you had to cut a feature from the plan, how did you make that call?

Do you use a scoring framework? If yes, do you trust the output, or do you override it based on gut?

Think of a time you prioritized something that scored low but still felt right. What made you override the score?

03

Pain Points

What's the most frustrating part of prioritization today?

Where do decisions get delayed the most? Missing data, too many opinions, or something else?

What kind of information is hardest to gather when you're scoring a feature?

What's one thing about planning you've just accepted as normal but wish was different?

04

AI & Automation

If AI could handle one part of your planning process, what would you hand off first?

Where would you still want full control, even if AI could do it?

What would make you trust an automated prioritization system? What would break that trust?

Have you tried using ChatGPT or similar for planning? What worked and what didn't?

05

Collaboration & Stakeholders

Who else is involved in your quarterly planning? Eng leads, design, execs?

When there's a disagreement about priorities, how does it get resolved? Who has the final call?

How do you communicate the plan to the rest of the team? What format does it end up in?
